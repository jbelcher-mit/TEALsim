Inverter Magnetic Configuration:

The Inverter Magnet is a simulation consisting of a central magnet and one satellite magnet, both of which are in the same plane and are fixed in place. The magnetic moment of the satellite magnet is significantly smaller than that of the central magnet. A third magnet, identical to the central magnet, is free to move, but is confined to move along the line joining the first two magnets.

The central magnet and the moving magnet have their poles oriented in the same direction, and thus repel. The satellite magnet has its pole in the same direction but opposite to that of the central and moving magnet, and thus is attracted to both.

The moving magnet is attracted to the central magnet but repelled by the satellite magnet. The friction is initially set to zero. If you increase the friction you will see the moving magnet settle into an equilibrium position.
