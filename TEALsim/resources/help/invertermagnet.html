Inverter Magnetic Configuration:

The Inverter Magnet is a simulation consisting of a central magnet surrounded by six satellite magnets, all of which in the same plane and are fixed in place. The magnetic moments of the satellite magnets are significantly smaller than that of the central magnet. An eighth magnet, identical to the central magnet, is free to move, but is confined to move in the plane of the other seven magnets.



The central magnet and the moving magnet have their poles oriented in the same direction, and thus repel. The satellite magnets have their poles in the same direction but opposite to that of the central and moving magnet, and thus are attracted to both.

The moving magnet is attracted to the central magnet but repelled by the satellite magnets. The friction is initially set to zero. If you increase the friction you will see the moving magnet settle into an equilibrium position.
