Magnetostatic Pendulum Same Pole:

The Magnetostatic Pendulum Same Pole is a simulation consisting of one magnetic dipole that is free to move swinging on a pendulum and one stationary magnet dipole on the plate below the pendulum. The dipoles are both vertical but opposite in orientation, so that the same poles are facing at the bottom of the swing. You can change the magnetic moment of the swinging dipole compared to the stationary magnet, but only do that when the pendulum is just at the top of its arc, otherwise energy will not be conserved through the swing.

Use the Start button to start simulation. Use Pause button to pause simulation. Use the slider in the panel labeled "Ratio |m/M|" to change the dipole moment of the swinging dipole.

The physical parameters of this pendulum are as follows: the height of the support is 25 cm, and the length of the pendulum is 20 cm. The mass of the bob with the swinging magnet is 5 grams. The magnet moment of the fixed magnet is 10 A m^2.

Problem: Given these parameters, use conservation of energy to determine the ratio |m/M| so that the swinging magnet comes to rest at the bottom of the swing. The potential energy of a magnetic dipole is the negative of the dipole moment vector dotted into the local magnetic field. Answer:1.225
